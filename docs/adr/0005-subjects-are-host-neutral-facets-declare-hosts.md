# Subjects are host-neutral; Facets declare their Hosts

A **Host** is the runtime/container that renders a Facet and exposes it to agents through a Target dialect: a **Browser Host** renders in a browser session and is addressed as `browser:<session>/<ref>`; a **Canvas Host** renders in an AOS canvas and is addressed as `canvas:<canvas-id>/<ref>`. **Subjects are host-neutral** — Sigil, a radial menu, a Work Record, a 3D item all retain a single durable identity regardless of where they're rendered. **Facets** are what declare Host support, and a single Facet may have multiple Host implementations (e.g. a markdown narrative Facet with both a browser-hosted reader and a canvas-hosted reader).

We rejected attaching Host to the Subject (would fragment Subject identity into "browser-Sigil" vs "canvas-Sigil") and rejected a single `facets[].host` enum (`"browser" | "canvas" | "either"`) as too coarse — Facets need to express per-Host entry points and dialects, not just a flag. The schema direction is `facets[].hosts[]` of `{ kind, target_dialect, entry, ... }`, sketched in `shared/schemas/aos-workbench-subject-vnext.md`. **Browser-First** is a design posture for wiki/editor/artifact workbenches; **AOS-Native** is a strict requirement for Facets that depend on AOS runtime privileges (DesktopWorld, Surface Inspector, input routing diagnostics, readiness/permission surfaces).
