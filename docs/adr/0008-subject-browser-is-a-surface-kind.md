# Subject Browser is a class of surfaces, not the wiki renamed

A **Subject Browser** is any surface — Browser-Hosted or Canvas-Hosted — that opens Subject Entry Handles, inspects the Subject in focus, views and opens its Facets, follows Subject References, and maintains a Navigation Trail. The wiki is the first Subject Browser instance (browser-hosted, narrative-Markdown-default, wiki-rooted scope); Surface Inspector may expose AOS-Native Subject Browser behavior when navigating live runtime Subjects; a future Work Record browser would be a third. Subject Browsers *navigate* Subjects but do not own them — Subject identity and source of truth stay with the underlying source (wiki path, runtime registry, audit store, artifact bundle, etc.).

We rejected naming the wiki "the Subject Browser" (collapses the abstraction; the next runtime/audit browser would force a rename) and rejected reducing Subject Browser to a per-Facet capability (the repeated UX shell — search, open, trail, reference following, Facet selection — is substantial enough to deserve a surface kind). Phase 4 of `aos-grand-unification-plan.md` should be retitled "Browser-Hosted Wiki Subject Browser" to disambiguate the doubled "browser" (one is the Host, one is the Subject Browser surface kind); Phase 7 should note that Surface Inspector *may* implement AOS-Native Subject Browser behavior for runtime Subjects without forcing every diagnostic panel into the full surface contract.
