# Recipe, Playbook, and Workflow are three distinct artifacts on different scopes

**Recipe** is a reusable bounded procedure: how a piece of work might happen again. The repo has two flavors — documentation-only Recipes under `docs/recipes/` (Markdown SOPs that shape judgment) and source-backed Recipes runnable through AOS surfaces such as `aos ops` (re-executable knowledge with explicit inputs/outputs). **Playbook** is the agent-operable variant of a Recipe, structured around the `see → resolve → do → see → verify` shape with preconditions, target-resolution, postconditions, and repair hints. **Workflow** is an orchestration graph or chain that invokes Recipes, Playbooks, agent tasks, approvals, and external operations, often crossing systems or human gates. All three are Subjects.

Composition: Workflows may invoke Recipes or Playbooks; Recipes may compile into Playbook-like executable steps; Playbooks should not invoke Workflows (anything orchestrating multi-system gates and child runs *is* a Workflow). All three can be the origin of a Work Record: `origin: { kind: "ad_hoc" | "recipe" | "playbook" | "workflow", ref: <handle> | null }`. Documentation-only Recipes that guided a run without executing are cited via a separate `references[]` array with `relationship: "guided_by"`, not as origin.

We rejected collapsing Workflow and Playbook into one artifact at different abstraction levels (loses the audience/scope distinction — a Workflow with Slack approvals and external jobs is doing fundamentally different work than a Playbook clicking elements and asserting state) and rejected dropping Recipe in favor of Playbook-only (loses the existing repo vocabulary and the docs-only flavor that genuinely doesn't execute).
