# Claims and Postconditions split along the intent / execution boundary

A **Claim** is a durable, agent/human-readable assertion on a Work Record about what the run accomplished and lives on the **intent spine**. A **Postcondition** is a structured, machine-checkable condition tied to a `see` capture (DOM/AX/canvas state, artifact presence, file contents, command exit, etc.) and lives on the **execution map**, where it is *repairable* — selector or ref drift patches the Postcondition, never the Claim. A Claim references zero or more Postconditions; a Postcondition may also exist as a step-local gate inside a transitional `aos.playbook_step` descriptor without being promoted to a Claim. The verifier produces one **Claim Result** per Claim by evaluating its referenced Postconditions against captured Evidence; aggregated Claim Results determine the run's overall **Verifier Health**.

We rejected the alternative of treating "step-local claim" and "run-wide claim" as the only distinction (collapses the durable-vs-repairable axis the AOS Work Record model is built on) and rejected making Claims and Postconditions uniform (would mean editing a drifted selector looks like editing the Claim, violating the intent-spine contract). The Verifier Report's source of truth is `claim_results[]`; the plan's parallel `verified / failed / unverified` fields, if retained, are derived indexes of Claim IDs, not independent storage.
