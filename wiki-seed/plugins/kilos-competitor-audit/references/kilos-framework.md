# KILOS Framework Reference

© Symphony Talent, LLC, 2023. All rights reserved.

The KILOS model is Symphony Talent's proprietary framework for assessing employer brand motivations. It underpins all research activities — surveys, focus groups, competitor audits, and EVP development. It identifies the nuances of employee and candidate motivations that go beyond pay to create a differentiated emotional reason to work for an organization.

---

## The Five KILOS Pillars

### K — Kinship (Orange)
*Culture, community, DEI, psychological safety, wellbeing*

The degree to which employees feel they belong and are valued as people, not just workers.

**Sub-themes:**
- **Diverse and Inclusive** — People act in an understanding, welcoming, inclusive way toward all diversity
- **Wellbeing** — The company recognizes people as individuals; human first, employees second
- **Safe to voice opinions** — People can disagree openly or share alternate viewpoints without repercussions
- **Fairness and Respect** — People treat each other fairly and with respect regardless of seniority
- **Sense of Belonging** — A connection between colleagues that goes deeper than work, based on shared values/interests
- **Employee Testimonials** — Real employees share their lived experience of the culture
- **Culture/Values** — The company's values and ways of working are visible and distinctive
- **Collaboration/teamwork** — Working together across teams and functions is emphasized

**Messaging signals to look for:** DEI campaigns, employee stories, belonging language, community initiatives, culture pages, "we" and "us" framing, mental health and wellbeing benefits

---

### I — Impact (Purple)
*Customer-focused, internal influence, sustainability, innovation*

The degree to which employees feel their work makes a meaningful difference — for patients, society, the environment, or the business.

**Sub-themes:**
- **For people, society, communities** — Work has a direct positive impact on the people and communities served
- **On environment or planet** — Employees contribute to environmental sustainability
- **Empowerment/autonomy** — People are given freedom to choose how they do their work
- **Influence strategy / business direction** — People have opportunities to shape the direction of the business
- **Impact on a big/global scale** — Work contributes to solving society's big problems or large-scale projects
- **Innovation and invention** — People can define a vision for the future and take risks to achieve it
- **Transformation and change** — The company is undergoing or enabling meaningful transformation

**Messaging signals to look for:** Patient stories, innovation labs, R&D emphasis, sustainability reports, leadership messaging, "change the world" or "transform lives" language

---

### L — Lifestyle (Blue)
*Job security, benefits, work-life balance, flexibility, tools*

The practical and tangible conditions of work that affect day-to-day quality of life.

**Sub-themes:**
- **Good benefits/perks** — Competitive retirement, healthcare, share options, and other financial benefits
- **Clean, safe, attractive work environment** — The physical workplace is appealing and well-maintained
- **Policies** — Modern, relevant policies (parental leave, vacation, wellbeing support)
- **Flexibility (hours)** — Freedom to choose when to work
- **Flexibility (location)** — Remote, hybrid, or multi-site working options
- **Healthy work-life balance** — Encouragement to maintain well-being outside work
- **Job security/stability** — Stable market position; employees feel financially secure
- **Tools and resources/tech** — Access to cutting-edge tools, resources, and technologies

**Messaging signals to look for:** Benefits pages, hybrid work policies, "all well" programs, health/wellness perks, parental leave, flexible working campaigns

---

### O — Opportunity (Green)
*Skills attainment/development, internal mobility, advancement*

The degree to which employees can grow, advance, and evolve in their careers.

**Sub-themes:**
- **Skills development** — Opportunities to learn new skills and gain knowledge
- **Develop your professional expertise** — Deepening specialist skills, qualifications, or functional knowledge
- **Challenge/stretch** — Being pushed to expand remit and responsibilities
- **Internal mobility/choice of locations** — Ability to move function, role, country, or try new experiences
- **Variety and breadth of tasks** — Working on diverse tasks and projects where no two days are the same
- **Career progression/opportunities** — A clear pathway to more senior or leadership positions

**Messaging signals to look for:** Learning academies, career development pages, internal mobility programs, mentorship, leadership pipelines, "grow with us" language, Sanofi University-type programs

---

### S — Status (Wine/Maroon)
*Heritage, ethics, reputation, commercial success, recognition*

The degree to which being associated with the company enhances an employee's own standing and reputation.

**Sub-themes:**
- **Recognisable brand** — A well-known brand name that impresses others outside work
- **Industry reputation/market position** — A strong industry reputation for excellence, innovation, or commercial leadership
- **Long heritage/legacy** — A storied history and track record
- **Recognition as an employer** — Employer awards, rankings, certifications (e.g., LinkedIn Top Companies, Great Place to Work)
- **Voice for societal issues** — The company takes a public stand on social or ethical issues
- **Ethics** — A strong commitment to ethical conduct, safety, or corporate responsibility

**Messaging signals to look for:** Awards and rankings prominently displayed, industry leadership claims, heritage timelines, ethical commitments, ESG reports referenced on careers pages

## Brand Archetypes

When assessing each company, assign a primary (and optionally secondary) brand archetype based on the dominant messaging themes and tone.

| Archetype | Core drive | Key messaging signals |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Magician** | Transformation; making the impossible possible | "Transform", "reinvent", "discover", "innovate beyond limits", "work wonders" |
| **Hero** | Courage; overcoming difficulty to do what's right | "Make a difference", "take on challenges", "change lives", "impact millions", "do what matters" |
| **Ruler** | Leadership; order and excellence | "Lead", "world-class", "industry leader", "#1", "recognized as the best" |
| **Creator** | Originality; building something new and meaningful | "Diverse minds", "bold disruptors", "reimagine", "design the future", "create" |
| **Citizen** | Belonging; community and doing right by everyone | "Everyone belongs", "inclusive community", "for people like you", "we're in this together" |
| **Caregiver** | Nurture; helping and protecting others | "We care", "support you", "your wellbeing matters", "you're not alone here" |
| **Explorer** | Freedom; discovery and new experiences | "Explore", "adventurous", "no two days the same", "work anywhere", "uncharted territory" |
| **Sage** | Wisdom; expertise and learning | "Experts", "knowledge", "thought leadership", "grow your mastery", "learn from the best" |

## Messaging Theme Classification — Quick Reference

When reviewing careers website or social media content, classify each message into one of the 26 sub-themes above. Apply these principles:

1. **Use the verbatim content** — classify based on what is explicitly stated, not inferred
2. **One message can map to multiple themes** — e.g., "We offer flexible working and industry-leading benefits" maps to both Flexibility (L) and Good benefits (L)
3. **SSC-specific messaging** — note separately if messaging is specific to a Shared Service Centre, Hub, or regional function
4. **Social media only (SM)** — if a theme appears only on social media and not on the careers website, flag it as "SM only"
5. **Strength of signal** — a full section with multiple examples is a stronger signal than a single passing mention; use your judgment to distinguish primary vs. peripheral themes
