# Employer Brand Comparison

An Employer Brand Comparison is the multi-company artifact produced after at least two [Employer Brand Profiles](employer-brand-profile.md) have been synthesized. It explains how the client and competitors position themselves relative to each other.

## Minimum Contents

- Comparison scope and competitor set
- Cross-company message matrix
- Shared themes and overused claims
- Distinctive proof points
- Whitespace and opportunity areas
- Risks, conflicts, and evidence gaps

## Comparison Rules

- Compare like with like. If scope differs across companies, call that out before drawing conclusions.
- Separate message presence from message quality.
- Distinguish direct evidence from synthesis.
- Preserve uncertainty when evidence is incomplete or stale.

## Core Sections

### 1. Executive Readout

What matters most for a leader who will not read the full evidence set.

### 2. Company Snapshots

One concise block per company capturing positioning, strengths, and weaknesses.

### 3. Dimension Matrix

Use the agreed scoring scale from [Employer Brand Scoring Scale](../concepts/employer-brand-scoring-scale.md).

### 4. Competitive Interpretation

Explain what the matrix means, not just what it says.

### 5. Opportunity Calls

Identify whitespace the client can credibly own.

### 6. Evidence Gaps

Show where weak collection quality or missing surfaces make the comparison less reliable.
