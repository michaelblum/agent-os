# Employer Brand Profile

An Employer Brand Profile is the normalized single-company artifact for this workflow set. It turns collected evidence into a reusable point of view about how one company positions itself as an employer.

## Minimum Contents

- Company identity and scope
- Evidence coverage summary
- Employer value proposition summary
- Message-dimension analysis
- Brand voice notes
- Proof points and evidence citations
- Gaps, contradictions, and open questions

## Required Sections

### 1. Scope

Record the company, market, target audience, channels reviewed, and the date window of the evidence.

### 2. Evidence Coverage

State what was reviewed and what was not reviewed. A weak profile is still usable if the gaps are explicit.

### 3. Value Proposition

Summarize the core employer promise in plain language. Separate direct claims from analyst inference.

### 4. Message Dimensions

Map the brand to the chosen message framework. The default workflow uses [Employer Brand Message Dimensions](../concepts/employer-brand-message-dimensions.md).

### 5. Voice And Tone

Describe how the company sounds, not just what it says.

### 6. Proof

Capture the proof structure behind the claims: quotes, awards, benefits, employee stories, review sentiment, and visual evidence.

### 7. Open Unknowns

List what the profile cannot yet support.

## Rules

- Every strong claim should point to evidence.
- Evidence gaps must be preserved, not hidden.
- The profile should be reusable without the original analyst present.
- Comparison work should consume profiles, not raw notes, wherever possible.
